# Module Catalogue

## Year 1

### IT1751 — Computing Mathematics 1 *(60 hours)*
Equips students with the fundamentals of computing mathematics and calculus, forming the foundation for more advanced mathematics topics. Topics covered include number system, set theory and logic, matrices, relations, functions, differentiation and integration.

---

### IT1753 — Principles of Computing *(60 hours)*
Introduces a structured approach to problem-solving independent of computer type or language. Students are taught to plan and describe program logic using flow charts and pseudo codes. Programming fundamentals such as algorithm, logic, computer representation of information, variables and data types are covered.

---

### IT1768 — Database Systems *(60 hours)*
Covers the relational model in database. Describes the fundamentals of database systems, including the logical and physical design in a relational database. Introduces Structured Query Language (SQL) to facilitate data definition, retrieval, and manipulation together with database security & administration.

---

### IT1770 — Communication Skills 1 *(30 hours)*
Teaches students to communicate effectively and skillfully in an academic and professional environment. Explores effective communication, reading, listening, technical writing, proposal writing and oral presentation. Helps students develop good communication strategies to improve interpersonal and teamwork skills.

---

### ITP171 — IT Fundamentals & Project *(120 hours)*
Equips students with a broad understanding of the enabling information and communication technologies powering the infrastructure in today's connected world. Students will learn about the organisation and functionalities of hardware components working together with IT software and tools in a typical computer system. Covers computer security, networks and communications, servers and storage as well as software application tools for creating media rich web sites. Students will undertake a project to explore ideas in applying their IT knowledge and skills to implement innovative web applications for engineering and business.

---

### IT1761 — Computing Mathematics 2 *(60 hours)*
Covers basic statistical concepts and applications. Topics include descriptive statistics, linear regression and correlation, permutation and combination, probability theory and probability distributions, serving as the foundation for subsequent topics such as estimation and hypothesis testing.

---

### IT1762 — Data Structures and Algorithms *(60 hours)*
Covers concepts of data structures and algorithms for effective problem-solving and implementation of computer programs. Topics include dynamic data structures such as stacks, queues, linked lists and trees. Algorithms for searching and sorting are also covered.

---

### IT1769 — Laws & Ethics of IT *(30 hours)*
Provides students with an awareness of the ethics and law of IT. Covers issues like intellectual property rights protection and infringement, copyright and plagiarism, software piracy, computer crimes, Internet fraud, objectionable materials, and confidentiality in the information age. Singapore's laws on copyright, computer misuse and electronic transactions are covered at an awareness level.

---

### IT1773 — Infocomm Security Fundamentals *(60 hours)*
Provides students with the foundation of information security concepts such as confidentiality, integrity, availability as well as risks and controls to mitigate risks. Other topics include identification and authentication, authorisation and access control, auditing and accountability, cryptography and a brief introduction to operating system security, application security and operations security.

---

### ITP172 — Networking Fundamentals & Project *(120 hours)*
Incorporates a new approach to teaching networking fundamentals while incorporating critical business and financial skills to equip students for entrepreneurship opportunities in networking business scenarios. Networking topics include network terminology and protocols, local area networks, wide area networks, OSI, transmission media, Internetworking devices, Ethernet technologies and switching, TCP/IP protocol suites, IP addressing, packet analysis and network standards. Students will undertake a project to develop an innovative networking business proposal applying their networking, business and financial skills.

---

## Year 2

### IT2755 — Software Engineering *(60 hours)*
Begins with a formal definition of software engineering. The software development lifecycle is elaborated and serves as a guide to a maturing engineering discipline. Related issues such as software quality and configuration management are introduced. Various software testing techniques will be covered for software validation. Project management techniques such as project planning and control are also taught.

---

### IT2757 — Server & Services Security Management *(60 hours)*
Provides students with a good understanding in provisioning and management of secured server systems, network services and hosting environments. Covers secured configuration, system hardening, access and activity authentication, authorisation and monitoring of web and application servers, database servers, DNS, DHCP, email servers and hosting environments such as Windows and UNIX operating systems in physical or virtualised platforms.

---

### IT2758 — Operating Systems *(30 hours)*
Covers the fundamental concepts of operating systems with a focus on important features and functions. Topics include management of memory space and peripheral devices, file management, system security requirements and system administration. Students will also be exposed to different types of operating systems such as Linux and Windows.

---

### IT2762 — Networking and Communications *(60 hours)*
Covers routing and switches technology, routing theory and router configuration. Includes routing concepts, routing protocols, distance vector and link state routing theory, routing loop issues, router configuration and troubleshooting, access lists, classless routing, advanced routing protocols, switching concepts and configuration, virtual LAN, spanning tree protocols and virtual trunking protocol. Students will get hands-on experience configuring routers and switches.

---

### ITP271 — Web Application Development & Project *(120 hours)*
Provides students with knowledge and skills to develop web applications using the latest web technologies. Covers web application architecture and various issues related to web application development. Students will learn to create dynamic and interactive web pages using server-side programming, web forms and web server controls, state management, security and debugging, and database access techniques. Students will undertake a project applying Internet technologies and web skills to create interactive websites.

---

### IT2768 — IT Services and Management *(30 hours)*
Provides insights into the latest developments in IT service management. Enables students to apply methodologies regarding the planning, support, delivery and management of quality IT services. Covers concepts, terms, definitions, objectives, benefits and relationships within core IT service management processes and functions according to best practices frameworks.

---

### IT2771 — Rich Internet Application Development *(60 hours)*
Equips students with the capability to develop Rich Internet Applications (RIA), enabling the deployment of content-rich web applications over the Internet with the power of traditional desktop applications. Students will learn to develop RIA applications targeted for multiple platforms such as mobile and rich web applications. Covers evolving standards such as HTML5/CSS3, including support for drag-and-drop, video and animation.

---

### IT2774 — Network Security *(60 hours)*
Provides students with fundamentals of network security, emphasising the role of data communications and encryption. Covers network security, compliance and operation security, threats and vulnerabilities, and application, data and host security. Other topics include access control, firewalls, VPN, intrusion detection systems, identity management and cryptography.

---

### IT2775 — Operations Security *(60 hours)*
Covers the protection and controls of information processing assets in an enterprise IT environment. Topics include operations management practices such as user management, access control, patch management, malware defence, configuration and change management, backup and recovery, personnel management, physical security, single sign-on, identity management, multi-factor authentication and access auditing. Also includes common practices in contractor/product selection, outsourcing security services, managed security and continuous security monitoring.

---

### ITP272 — Sensor Technologies & Project *(120 hours)*
Provides students with a broad coverage of sensor technologies, their applications and the basic analog and digital support circuitry required for interfacing to sensors. Students learn to select various types of sensors and program smart objects to collect and process data. Students will undertake a project applying IT, sensor and hardware interfacing knowledge to create applications used in business and engineering environments.

---

## Year 3

### IT3772 — Communication Skills 2 *(30 hours)*
Develops critical communication and interactive skills needed to prepare students for entry into the job market as working professionals. Focuses on interpersonal skills and business communication in the workplace. Topics include business correspondence, writing resumes, interviewing skills, conflict management and negotiation skills.

---

### IT3779 — Smart Object Technologies *(60 hours)*
Equips students with knowledge of developing solutions to solve real-life problems by exploiting the latest technologies in smart objects or devices such as wireless sensor networks, ambient devices and RFID tagged objects connected over the Internet. Students will learn to develop and deploy applications on smart objects. Covers supervisory control and data acquisition technologies and data analytics for performing analysis on raw data.

---

### IT3784 — Computer & Network Forensics *(60 hours)*
Equips students with skills and knowledge in cyberattack planning, prevention, detection, response and investigation with the goals of counteracting cybercrimes and making responsible persons/groups accountable. Topics include fundamentals of digital forensics, forensic data acquisition and analysis, network surveillance, intrusion detection and response, incident response, anti-forensics techniques, anonymity and pseudonymity.

---

### IT3789 — Cyber Security Attack and Defence *(60 hours)*
Covers the concepts, tools and techniques used in both cyberattacks and defence. Topics include foot-printing and social engineering, scanning and enumeration, system and network penetration, reverse engineering, planting Trojans, backdoors and escalating attacks from compromised systems. Students will also learn the penetration testing process and the ethical and legal aspects.

---

### ITP374 — Systems & Network Security Project *(120 hours)*
Provides students with a sound understanding of the core security concepts and skills needed for installation, troubleshooting and monitoring of secured server systems, network services and devices. Covers in-depth theoretical and practical understanding of network security principles, tools and configurations for designing, implementing and supporting system and network security. Topics include AAA, advanced firewall technologies, Intrusion Prevention System, LAN security, VPN and remote access services, DDOS detection and mitigation, system and security policy, web security and other emerging topics. Students will also undertake a project using the knowledge and skills acquired.

---

### IT3768 — Internship Programme *(24 weeks)*
A 24-week internship programme at a company that exposes students to a real-life work environment through a structured and integrated learning programme. Students will apply the knowledge and skills acquired to work practice, gain a broader perspective of the industry, companies and careers in their respective professions, and deepen relevant skills. Students will experience the realities of a work environment and the importance of work values and culture.

5 Ways to increase FPS in games

Want your PC games to run faster and smoother without spending money? Here are 5 simple tips gaming framerates.

1. Upgrade your Graphics Drivers to the latest versions

Your graphics card is the centerpiece of your gaming experience, but it can’t unleash its true performance without the proper software installed on your PC or laptop. Going from the driver that came either with your PC or with your graphics card to the latest version can make a large performance difference.

Nvidia GeForce owners:
1. Go to Nvidia's Website.
2. Select the Product Type, Product Series, Product, Operating System and Language.
3. Press “Search”.
4. Press “Download”
5. When the driver is downloaded run the .exe file and if you are prompted by a warning message click on “Allow”.
6. Follow the setup process.
7. Restart your computer

ATI Radeon owners:
1. Go to ATI's Website
2. Select the Type of System, Product family, Product and Operating System you have.
3. Click on “Display Results”.
4. Download and Run the file.
5. Restart your computer.

Intel video card owners:
1. Visit Intel’s download page.
2. Find the product by category choosing the product’s family, line and name

2. Overclock Your GPU

As a last ditch effort for some users who have an extremely outdated graphics card, or as an option for any game who just wants to get higher framerates, one way that you can increase your FPS is to overclock your system’s hardware. You’ll first want to try overclocking your GPU.

You can overclock your GPU with provided software from GPU manufacturers like MSI Afterburner.

3. Turn Down the Game’s Graphics Settings

If you’re getting really low FPS in a game, one of the easiest and quickest fixes you can make is simply to go into the game’s settings and lower the graphics settings. You can either do this manually and focus on turning down the most intensive graphics settings first, or you can use the preset options in the game’s menu to turn all of the settings down as low as possible.

Settings like shadows, view distance, anti-aliasing and textures that can provide a big boost to your FPS if they are turned off.

If you’re hovering around 20-30 FPS in your game, you might want to go through ever graphics setting and lower them a bit, and re-lauch your game to see what kind of increase it gave you. And, then do that until you can get to a framerate that is more acceptable for you.

4. Use optimisation software

Lots of programs run in the background in Windows, and they use up resources including memory and CPU time. You can use PC optimisation utilities to ensure there are no superfluous processes running that could hamper game performance.

Windows includes some optimisation tools, but you can also try Razer Cortex, which does a lot of the hard work for you, and is compatible with Windows 7, 8, and 10.

5. Reduce your resolution

You can also enhance FPS rates by lowering graphics settings. Reducing the display resolution may increase FPS rates depending on your hardware. In other words, this is not a universal recipe, but you should test it to see if it works in your case.

In most cases, lowering resolution will speed up the gameplay. The best solution is to adjust resolution using games’ graphics settings.
